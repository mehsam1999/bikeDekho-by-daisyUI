Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Check your helmet for visible damage, wear, or if it's over 3-5 years old. If it has been in an impact or no longer fits snugly, it's time to replace it. Always follow the manufacturer’s guidelines for helmet lifespan.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding a bike that's been in storage, inspect the tires for cracks or flat spots, check the brakes and gears for proper function, and ensure the chain is lubricated. If any parts show wear or damage, consider a tune-up before riding.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding your bike, always follow traffic signals, ride with the flow of traffic, and use hand signals for turns. Wear a helmet, stay visible with bright clothing or lights, and be mindful of pedestrians and other vehicles.
